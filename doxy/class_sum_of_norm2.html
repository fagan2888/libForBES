Detailed Description

Sum of 2-norms.

Version
version 0.1
Date
Created on November 6, 2015, 1:23 AM
Author
Pantelis Sopasakis

Let \(X \cong \mathbb{R}^n\) be a vector space and let \(k\in\mathbb{N}\) be an integer which divides \(n\) exactly.

Let us denote \(m=n/k\). Then,

\[ X = \prod_{i=1}^{m}X_i, \]

where \(X_i\cong \mathbb{R}^{k}\), all equipped with the norm \(\mu \|\cdot\|_2\) (for some constant scalar \(\mu>0\)).

Then, space \(X\) is equipped with the norm

\[ \|x\| = \sum_{i=1}^{k} \mu\|x_{(i)}\|_2. \]

This is indeed a norm in \(X\) and makes the space complete.

There is also an inner product we may associate with space \(X\), that is

\[ \langle x,y\rangle = \mu \sum_{i=1}^{k} x_{(i)}^{\top}y_{(i)}. \]

The above norm, known as sum of Euclidean norms defines the dual norm

\[ \|x\|_* = \frac{1}{\mu} \max \{\|x_{(1)}\|_2, \|x_{(2)}\|_2, \ldots, \|x_{(m)}\|_2\}, \]

where \(x_{(i)}\in\mathbb{R}^k\) is the part of \(x\) including its components from \((i-1)k+1\) to \(ik-1\).

Note
This implementation assumes an evenly distributed partitioning of \(X\). This may be restrictive in many applications and the class SeparableSum should be used instead.
This function is used in group LASSO problems.

\[ (\mathrm{prox}_{\gamma f}(v))_{(i)} = \left[1-\frac{\gamma\mu}{\|v_{(i)}\|_2}\right]_{+}v_{(i)} \]

\[ \sum_{i=1}^{n/k} \left[1-\frac{\gamma\mu}{\|v_{(i)}\|_2}\right]_{+} \|v_{(i)}\|_2 \]
